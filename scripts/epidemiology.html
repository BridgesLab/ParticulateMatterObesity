Combined Data

The data were not normally distributed (age adjusted obesity, p=6.5465 × 10-10; particulate matter levels p=1.1425 × 10-18), so we calculated the correlation coefficient using Spearman's Rank-Based test, which yielded rho=0.1093, rho2=0.0119 and p=0.0187

Quintile Analysis

We looked at the differences between the upper and lower quintiles of PM10 levels in 1990, and found no difference between the age adjusted obesity rates between these groups by a Student's T-Test (p=0.3913). We did a Student's T-Test because both quintiles had a normal distribution (p=0.1399 and 0.7378 respectively by Shapiro-Wilk Test), and equal variance (p=0.3126 via Levene's Test).
